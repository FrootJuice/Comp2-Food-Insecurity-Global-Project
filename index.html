What Is College Food Insecurity?

Food insecurity among college students means not having reliable access to affordable, nutritious food. At Northwest Missouri State University, this affects more students than many people realize. It's not just about being hungry between classes—it's about students making impossible choices between buying textbooks, paying rent, or eating regular meals.

College food insecurity looks different than you might expect. It includes students skipping meals to save money, relying on ramen noodles for weeks at a time, visiting campus food pantries, or worrying constantly about where their next meal will come from while trying to focus on their education.

The Reality at Northwest

42% College students experience food insecurity (El Zein et al., 2019)
30-50% US college students face hunger - higher than general population (Hagedorn-Hatfield et al., 2022)
30% More likely to drop out when food insecure (multiple studies)
15.8% Nodaway County residents are food insecure (Feeding America, 2023)

Maryville, Missouri's rural location adds unique challenges. Limited public transportation, fewer grocery options, and distance from family support systems mean Northwest students face particular obstacles in accessing affordable, nutritious food.

Breaking the Stigma

Using food assistance isn't a sign of failure—it's a smart use of available resources. Many successful alumni used campus food pantries during tough semesters. If you're struggling, you're not alone, and reaching out for help is a sign of strength, not weakness.

Get Help or Get Involved

Whether you need assistance or want to help fellow Bearcats, resources are available. No Northwest student should have to choose between their education and their next meal.
