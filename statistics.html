Academic Research on College Student Food Insecurity

Peer-reviewed studies examining the prevalence, causes, and impacts of food insecurity among college students.

Multi-Institutional Study: 42% of College Students Face Food Insecurity

El Zein et al. (2019) - BMC Public Health study surveying 3,097 students across eight universities, finding strong correlations between food insecurity and poor academic performance, physical health, and mental wellbeing.

Read Study

A Decade of College Student Hunger: National Review

Hagedorn-Hatfield et al. (2022) - Frontiers in Public Health comprehensive review showing 30-50% of US college students consistently experience food insecurity, higher than the general population. Examines trends, risk factors, and health consequences.

Read Review

Barriers to College Student Food Access (2024)

Landry et al. (2024) - Journal of Nutritional Science scoping review examining policies, systems, and environmental factors that prevent college students from accessing food, including transportation limitations and SNAP eligibility restrictions.

Read Review

Food Insecurity Among US College Students: Scoping Review

Nikolaus et al. (2020) - Advances in Nutrition review of 55 studies showing 9-65% variance in food insecurity rates among college students, with higher rates among minority students and those from low-income backgrounds.

Read Review
